I pause to observe

The faces on the train. In my bay of

Four seats, cell two by two, a selection

From the beginning dawn – countenances young

Through old – all seated in this small segment,

A carriage gallery, with the distance

And quiet of works of art: Old Man, Fat Man

And a maturing girl nurturing youth.

I watch with feeling.

 

   I glimpse a mirror,

The reflection from the carriage window;

 For a brief moment, I see a doubled

Image: Old Man is figured with objects

Rushing past outside – patches of sunlight,

Blurring tree lines, streaking urban sprawls.

My stomach flips with vertigo when I

Notice the girl sitting opposite me

Also lurks inside this dual reflection:

The spray of her freckles spins inside the

Fade of aging eyes and the quilt of the

Sunlight’s thread; her shell-shaped ear curls tightly

In the leading wisp of Old Man’s thin hair,

At times blending with snarls of clustered leaves

That stretch the hard length of their wind-mad dance

Across the crown of his sloping forehead.

But then, in the wake of the sun’s retreat,

As clouds gather density – darkening above

With misty meetings, refracting vapour –

A layer of shroud peels away the bright

And obvious sights of the world outside,

Leaving only an image of the two,

Twining a dusky mingle as the girl’s

Youthful expanse of clean neck swans sweetly

Close to the wrinkled tremble of Old Man’s

Broken lips. Her hips, swelling with promise

And future, sways fertile intimacy

In the Mirror World, smoothing arthritic

Hands that, spotted with brown age and twisting

Blues, a frail bloom of spider veins,

Have forgotten the warmth and velvet

Of a human touch, that remember only

Folding shut the slug-cold gaze of Diggers,

Gallipoli mates sunk in a wet suck –

Blood and mud.

 

                                           She perfumes,

Her breathing is rhythmic, the heft of her

Woman’s flesh weighing a regular press of

Rounded heart beat – one moment a pliant

Softness, the next a heated flush of curved

Firmness – along Old Man’s bird-weak spread

Of ribs brittle with the ache of seeped chill.

With the sun pale and tender-beamed in the

Meek of morning, and filtered by eddies

Of dewy cloud, the red burn heating the

Cresting horizon lies too low to spear

The lonely of the day’s illumination –

Its shine of buildings, concrete paths, garden

Lawns, trimmed trees, neat hedges and potted roads –

Through the dark quiver and sweet illusion of

Old Man and his girl all dressed in shadow.

In the private world of darkness and glass,

Old Man visions his dreaming of beauty;

I watch as the midnight heart of age sighs

A prayer inside the impossible touch

Of a dusking dawn.

 

                         My gaze moves on, pained.

A man, thickened by mounds of fleshy food,

Heats next to the girl, fat beading a stain

Of sweat across the limp line of his brow,

Glossy shine not yet evident in the

Pallor of the piping sun’s moment of

Sleepy lull. Fat Man always cooks for two:

Last night he cooked a five-pound leg of lamb,

Side served with carrot, potato and green beans.

He set two places like he always does,

Waited five minutes, hoping someone would

Knock on the door and ask him if he’d like

Appreciate some company tonight.

No one ever does.

 

With a lonely sigh,

Fat Man ends up eating both of the

Meals, small house silent but for a dextrous

Clicking, his utensils flashing in the

Pudge of plump fingers, dinner table much

Too large for the three hundred pound Fat Man,

Food clumping to his body as fat, size

Reaching for human companionship with

Loafing rings of lazy bulk, a jellied

Hug that repels - a carriage much to large

For an increasing mass, the Fat Man who

Fats to touch someone.

 

Normally, no one

Is willing to sit near him, disgusted

By his sweating hulk, the loud whistle of

Horse-flared nostrils, and the dun odour of

His desperate loneliness. This morning, 

The Slumber World touches.

 

    Fat Man’s abundance rolls

His bulge of human contact on to the

Slender arm of the girl; she does not seem

To notice the doughy warmth leaning the

Ease of its embrace along her feminine

Flank. His eyes are spaced far apart; his nose

Looks like it was stamped in children’s putty,

Flat, broad and uneven; his ears, drooping

Under the excess of a long skin fold,

Hang like stretched sheets of pasta; and his shirt

Is crumbed with breakfast toast and dustings of

Sugar. Fat Man’s fingers rest on the broad

Smile of his thighs, muscle tone seated with

The rhythms of the train’s motion, peaceful

Giggles of mass carrying a wealth of heart, 

Fat choofing at ease.

 

          Her lashes cascade,

Darkly sighing fine lines upon her snowy cheek;

The veil of dusk flickers the hush of shade

Like a drowsy poppy, seducing to sleep.

The girl’s head lolls to the side, mane of hair

Pouring a caress across Fat Man’s arm.

She mewls in the pillow of his presence,

Settling among the cushions of his fat,

Surrendering to the Slumber World and

Louring forgetfulness on the harsh day,

Melting her judgement and distaste in the

Warmth of a shading giant. Fat Man descants

With the rhythm of the carriage; the tone

Of his smiling thighs; the harmonies of

The dark; and the melodies of the girl’s

Parted petals whispering their soft snore,

And her blood humming its delicate strength

Under the cream of tissue paper skin.

 I watch as his under-sized jaw twists, ugly

Yet gentle, mouth a crush of too many teeth,

Shaping the expression of his joy as

His fat giggles night’s will-o’-the-wisp.

Fat Man lays his head sideways, satisfied:

She slumbers with him.

 

                    And this too pains me.

The four of us travel east, carrying

A stolen moment in vain; already

It begins to steam out of existence,

Vapours of the night succumbing to the

Murderous dawn. Soon the girl will stir, and

The glass will pierce with daylight’s shatter.

I observe, hurt in my breast, the idyll

Dwellers’ poetry – shadow and feeling – 

Bleed under the knifing day: these Men– lost.

 I watch the girl begin to stir, dragging

Her slumbering touch away from Fat Man’s

Song of happiness, clawing her image

Out from the intimate glass, sinking Old

Man’s dreaming, leaving only Memory’s drown.

With each rustle of pretty clothing,

Something precious dies.

 

I strengthen my sight,

Increase the span of my vision’s feeling:

The girl has never been more beautiful

Than she is now. She wreathes the majesty

Of destruction inside the moss of her

Awakening emerald, lid curtains

Fluttering open the glint of her Earth.

She weaves the doom of Slumber and Dreaming

With blinking vines, and binds them upon the

Green stone of her merciless eye. I gaze:

She takes my very breath.

 

                        The girl starts to rise;

I find her inside the storm of my eye

And she stills, returning my gaze, so quiet,

And it is recognition that shines in

Her emerald violence - I also majesty.

She sees the depths of my Sight, and I her

Beauty. Birds song the nearness of dawn,

But it is the song of our blood that burns.

I reach for her Earth as she reaches for

My Sky: so close, breath mingling, we journey

The breaking rise of the sun.
