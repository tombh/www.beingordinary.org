Beneath Notre Dame, washed with rain, we stood gazing. The Bells rung thick in the grey air and their heavy vibrations fell amongst the soaked pedestrians and the snaking and broken line of tourists who wore their black umberellas like a phalanx. Your wet hair clung to your cheeks and cascaded across your forehead like a black torrent flowing over the delicious cool of marble. I remember the faces of sodden stragglers, dashing by, laughing and huddled into their damp shoulders or squinting and pointing with extended arms as they crowded over ruined maps. "Ou est le Metro?"
