            I song with sun

.

Words wisp across my open sky while

Leaves dance in autumn twists and plastic bags.

A tall skyscraper of city apartments:

On top floor, a concrete hurry next to ocean soar.

.

I stand on my head, feet sipping nothingness,

Mouth relaxed, skin gentle.

.

Fond saliva has no form;

I dribble and pass it by,

Opening emptiness as I fly

My world in to a frozen concrete sky.

.

Saliva joins the ocean as I smile

Along the curve of rushing air:

I splash in the gravity of your home.

.

I empty, dance till my feet are thread,

Spin till my head flows as ink.

.

Summer leaves and winter falls,

Joyous notes curling in a gnarl of thread.

.

I float without a warning;

I warm gold in the morning.

.

I dawn with the scent of a wink,

A flash of colour in a smile, and speak to you;

I spring, weaving myself about your legs,

Trickling across stony parts;

I roll carpets, spun skies of embroidered ocean,

A babble of drifting creeks;

I cloud inside a broken smile,

Patter you a rain of leaf and feeling.

.

I sing my coils.

I dance my soul.

I lean my ease.

.

I slough and shed runaway feet;

I tread with garden in welcome pace.

.

My right hand is my left hand,

My left hand is my right hand;

A green spread of fingers,

It wreathes and lingers.

.

I celebrate the we self.

.

I nature for trees; I height for mountains.

I wet for oceans; I vast for skies.

I welkin as earth; I earth for creatures.

.

I creature my heart.

.

I trace with feather and flex of ambushing paw,

Twitch the tang of grass on the pink of a nose,

Glide a tail in pleasing circles,

Eddy the wink of dewy grass,

Ruffle a downy breath

And crop plantations:

Corn among the tufts of an ear,

Bananas sprouting along curved fingers,

Cotton groving in the fluff of flight.

.

I length a feline sharp to peel rich sugars,

Feed breezing currents the ring of happy bells.

.

I invite the rose to plume on a bee’s wing,

Tweet the mottle of honeying plenty,

Garden the legs bowing with rainbow sacks.

.

A cat leaps and a bird takes flight.

.

I lie in grass, dying.

.

I forget who I am.

.

An abundance of textures:

I shatter on the vision before me.                      

.

Identity bleeds.

.

A griffin walks across a grave.

.

Diaphanous sheets of me rustle foldings of liquid,

Lean with the seep of drinking fronds.

.

Slumbering in a strange breath,

I join a nymph of melody

As she swims the oasis of my depths.

.

I trill with the giggle of slender ankles,

Trestle a home inside the sparkle of silvered gaze,

Flutter my rhythms along the curling of her toes,

The flax of her luxuriant kick,

The daint of her hair.

.

Always losing myself, fracturing inside the Imaginary.

.

The piping of flutes lap against my shore,

Tide to the pound of frantic dancing.

.

Muscular feet stamp a rattle through my soul,

Storm clouds of dust,

Thicken thunder,

Grind a rattle along my throat.

.

The breaking of my soul reaches for a voice.

.

Inside oceans of feeling,

Geysers of knowledge,

Trenches of memory,

I can only hiss and shriek,

Lost in the splendour of my learning,

Imprisoned in footprints

Librarying a citadel of blood.

.

I am left me all alone.

.

I walk and know not where.

.

I exist and know not how.

.

I imagine and know not why.

.

I know and feel not what.

.

Pouring myself in to the past,

Filtering me through a mould,

I stream across my grave,

Rivulet in the moistures of dead soil,

Kiln the vessel contained within,

Clay my essence without a tongue to spake.

.

I hunger as the Earth names me Monster.

.

My spine arcs the tension of final moments,

Curves the thunders that roar inside my drowning heart;

Arms splayed on the heaven’s anvil,

Sky rips from my breaking lips an eagle’s cry.

.

I take wing;

I flee the bloodying earth.

.

I rush midnight under the might of my feather spread;

I scorch toward the sun.

.

Feathers peel raw in the flay of the wind’s kiss,

Bones break inside the caress of clouds,

Air bleeds upon my eye.

.

The heavens cast me down, pinioned and transformed.

.

I tear as the Sky names me Unwanted.

.

A broken leopard lying on its side,

Fur matted in clumps of disease;

I trickle my blood and twist, motionless,

With internal pain – the Sky eats me from within.

.

Once more, I try to speak to you:

The earth spears me with blades of grass,

Wreaks a roughness on my tongue,

Summers a plough of bladed hooves.

.

A feeble cry,

A mewling puddle of cat saliva,

A heavy-lidded meow.

.

Hurtful blows – the seasons roll on.

.

Life forges me,

Hurts me to make me.

.

Feline slits cinder beneath the sun.

.

I refuse to turn aside my gaze;

I burn my sight to the socket.

.

I make me.

.

I bruise as Death names me You.

.

I surge,

I rouse,

I rise my untameable,

I quest my enormity,

I cyclone my titanic love.

.

Against the strongest bonds of history,

The most certain restrictions of men,

The inevitability of rule,

The voice of the Fathers,

I chorus the wild of my veins.

.

I celebrate my blood.

.

I coil them in spools,

Pendent my vitality in colour,

Embed myself as iron diamonds,

Glitter my majesty in black oceans,

Law with all that I am.

.

I am Unbound.

.

Nothing can stop the blood of me.

.

The world celebrates my strength.

.

Maybe you can hear me red as I glow;

Maybe you can hear me emblem my breast.

.

I journey me through the Labyrinth,

A threaded way;

I skein upon my feet,

I glore with my opening thread.

.

I full my felt cloth,

Drape raiments in hidden places,

Ribbon the spider’s corner.

.

I home with sparrows,

Den with wolves,

Pride with lions,

Clicker with crickets,

Nest with hurt,

Time with age.

Lair with truth.

.

I do all these things and more.

.

I am not poetry for hard times;

I am poetry for all times.

.

I never sparse:

I love you too much.

.

I welcome even monsters.

.

I cradle sapphires,

Ruby lips,

Emerald leaves,

Diamond ears.

.

I do not mine the rocks;

I pass over everything in peace.

.

My breath is treasure enough;

My lungs vein the earth with wealth.

.

The rock mines me.

.

There is so much of me;

I give myself in handfuls.

.

Abundant is me;

I hold nothing back.

.

I gaggle with the geese overhead;

I busy with the ants underfoot;

I tuck with the slumbering ducks;

I bow with the splendid light;

I leaf with the dancing foliage;

I toil with the eager dirt;

I moon with the rhythms;

I hive with the stars;

I dust with the honey;

I warble with the philosopher;

I weave with the bark;

I silly with the saints;

I smoke with the warmth;

I lope with the wolf;

I combobulate with the cloud;

I mist with the grass;

I seat with the standing gulls;

I mound with the dunes.

.

My genius is the glory of the sun.

.

All values glitter on me, deep-spun on my looming soul.

.

Flowing golden leafs of me,

Pages and pages of me,

I gentle in the palm of your hand,

Tower health in ruined cottages,

Moss blessings in cracking mortar,

Spray volumes in the knell of dry wells.

.

I am for whom the bell tolls.

.

I lightning you my depths,

Crackle the arcs of my sway,

Glass the sandy heaths.

.

I in turn toll for you.

.

I unchamber you from the hour,

Soar the dapple of your deserts,

Depart the haste of your camels,

Child you the marvel of my eye.

.

I refract with you,

Expand your flow.

.

I prism delights upon the shade.

.

I melt your essence,

Teach the tears to flow.

.

I clothe time around your sole;

I splash me in your glistening soul.

.

I teach the slow of time,

My smile of we.

.

I quiet stalking darkness,

Free grains of sand,

Unshutter frowning curtains,

Coo silks of midnight,

Hoot a gliding embrace.

 

I enter the blank of the Bull’s eye.

.

I have found myself:

I know I am real.

.

Everywhere I dance:

Always with you,

For you,

As you.

.

I teach the dance.

.

You do not awkward.

Even when you stumble,

You are beautiful.

You can only grace.

.

I share my beauty.

.

To receive me is to be beautiful;

To not receive me is to destroy me.

.

I love how you dance:

The play of feet,

The wish of air,

The small of a back,

The stream of hair.

.

You texture so wonderful.

.

I look the sky to shape upon the earth,

Comb the shag of its garden,

The still of its ocean,

Through ripples of my feeling,

Tensing a blanket from

Whitest threads, bluest dyes,

On billows of wind.

.

I laugh away despairing gusts

To spread the contours

I hurl and laze.

.

I swell my big;

I tangle my small.

.

I glory.

.

No pain is too much.

.

I spasm my hand and gather you in the grass at dawn;

Shocks of bundled gold veil stubbles of green.

I rope all inside my blood and saliva,

Stain across the Sky and loam within the Earth.

.

All of this hurts.

.

Nothing diminishes my welcome.

.

As I lie dying, I smell you.

.

My nostrils smile in delight,

Frolic their hairs in greeting.

.

You scent me too.

.

Cascading a fountain of shaggy beard,

Blooming the wealth of crinkling eyes,

Snowing kind whiskers down a weathered rough,

I raise ancient mountains,

Clap rivers between my hands,

Rest my tender feet on the curve of a mountainous cheek.

.

I bubble galaxies of laughter;

My heart names me Everything.

.

I am very old and very young;

I was born out of time and in time.

.

Red sand creaks.

Jagged faith stares.

A saint despairs his Damascus.

 

Hush: you too are an apostle.

.

I heal even murderers.

.

Peace, brother.

.

Peace, sister.

.

Beating to the rhythm of internal splendour,

I gush whistles of blood –

Eagle, lion, leopard, cat, owl, griffin,

Titan, nymph, satyr, minotaur,

Horse, god, human, sparrow, All –

Humming the shapes

That moan for acceptance,

Vortex within their own fragility.

.

I am Poet and shear with awful plenty.

.

I strength for you.

.

I dance for you.

.

I cosmos for you.

.

All that I am sings to you, accepts you and is you.

.

I fleece with might,

Press the broken tower

Against my golden bell;

So soft I tint me in your tears,

Friend kisses of me beneath distressed lashes,

Empty age from weary bones.

.

I joy in the wonder of your eye,

Delicate my thumb on your sorrows.

.

The lines of my finger guide furrows,

Direct salts return to the great sea,

Tremble empathy on your petals.

.

I never plough;

I love my green.

.

I allow you to plough me.

.

No pain is too much.

.

I am convalescence;

I am health and good strength to you.

.

I glade my soul;

I invite pasture.

.

I clear my throat,

Vibrate the string of your harp;

I echo my vocal chords across Time.

.

Everyone shall hear me;

None can evade me.

.

I do not try to evade you.

.

You are magnificent.

.

I sound long and pure.

.

My medium is wonder;

I can only hum near you.

.

I mosaic the broken,

Texture the smile of jagged glass.

.

I turn toward the worst,

Knowing they are the best.

.

I shout a startle of hummingbirds,

Feather the perfection of imperfection.

.

I stand.

.

I am broad at the shoulders.

.

Many things stand with me.

.

I live with you.

I remember you.

.

I am beautiful.

You are beautiful too.

.

You recognise genius.

It is my smile.

.

Raining my sunbeams,

I warm the me that fears the storm,

Communicate you to the me that dreams

A we outside a tide of red lawn,

And cleft your lip to remind a tender sound.

.

My ‘yea’, an imaginary touch of real feeling,

Reaches, moon-beaming, across the summer dust.

.

My yea on the wind, I say,

My yea that dances with bees.

.

Echoing so broken,

No one hears me dying

As I speak to you

With braids of air.

.

I vibrate a shiver of flower motes,

Perfume along your spine.

.

I am always dying and always living.

.

I silent as I name myself to you.

.

And this is as it should be.

.

I hunger for food.

.

I tear for joy.

 

I bruise for warmth.

.

I silent for song.

.

I do all of this for you;

I have so many gifts for you,

Many, many mes for you.

.

One of these mes is you.

.

I steam with the minotaur,

Vapour blood and stink,

Lick a maze of wounds.

.

I fall with the rain,

Yield my happiness in glory,

Shout greetings to the ocean.

.

I decay with the leaves,

Detritus for tomorrow,

Spread my ancient lace.

.

I wind with the bees,

Gust golden notes of pollen,

Sound wonder in silence.

.

I celebrate my we.

.

I join you there so you can join we here;

I breathe my we and loaf to teach my slow.

.

I kiss slumbering feet and float the petal.

.

Sweetest of lies budding anew:

I sun on you, flower we true.

.

I invite breath;

I gesture soul.

.

I bubble tomorrow;

I soap yesterday.

.

I ring Earth;

I glory Now.

.

I halo my hello;

I world the we.

.

I dribble you a home inside the soil of my song.

.

I welcome as leaf, trail a gnarled perfume

Along the bark of a beach-playing Shepherd.

.

I laugh my shaggy hair; I brush my bristled earth.

I sand my car; I pant my tongue.

.

Never doubt I am welcoming you;

I await you everywhere,

Greet you always.

.

This too is me.

.

Am I not beautiful?

.

I depart but never leave;

I walk but never move.

.

I wake my night;

I birth my glow.

.

I leap, flying beyond bound of metal machine;

I court, sharing beyond bound with green on blue.

.

I sprig and palm flowers.

I skim and seat waterfalls.

I tilt and blow stars.

I jag and bend rainbows.

.

Everything twice and never: never me always.

 .

I touch eternity.

I grass.

.

I touch invisibility.

I cloud.

.

I touch humanity.

I moon.

.

I touch mortality.
I wolf.

.

I touch language.
I leaf.

.

I touch you.
I bee.

.

I touch me.

I we.

.

We are we.

.

Invisible I sight.

Silent I hear.

Alone I touch.

.

We are we.

.

Are we not beautiful says me?

.

I waft and ask: Do me understand this we?

I purr and ask: Do me feel this we?

I pad and ask: Do me know this we?

.

Everything asks about we;

We is the answer to me.

.

The we of my whole cares for each me.

.

I die for this we;

I birth for this we.

.

I green with sun.

.

I Earth my One.

.

I music with tree.

.

I song my we.
